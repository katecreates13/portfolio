01
Building a community moderation system from zero
3 brands, 1 platform, no documented process
+

The situation. Three home services brands shared one community platform with distinct audiences and voices. There was no documented process for handling provider posts, escalations, or violations consistently.

What I did. Reviewed every provider post line by line before it went live, built a moderation decision guide covering 7 trigger categories, standardized tone templates per brand, and built an escalation path — what I resolve myself versus what goes to product or support.

Outcome
  • Zero brand-voice inconsistencies across three distinct community spaces
  • Faster routing of recurring product issues to the right teams
  • Framework adopted as the team's standard operating process
02
Fixing inconsistent agent quality without a QA title
New hires, no reference standard, varying tone
+

The situation. Newly hired agents gave inconsistent responses — tone varied, accuracy was unreliable, and there was no single reference point for what "good" looked like.

What I did. Without being asked, reviewed new agents' responses daily with specific, kind, actionable feedback. Built master prompt templates for the most common scenarios — redundancy, billing disputes, no-shows — documenting the correct tone and resolution path for each.

Outcome
  • Measurable improvement in response consistency across the team
  • New agents ramped up faster with a concrete reference, not trial and error
  • Templates became a team-wide resource beyond onboarding
03
Turning frontline tickets into product fixes
30–50 daily interactions, same issues recurring
+

The situation. In a high-volume support queue, individual complaints got resolved in isolation. The same provider behaviors and product gaps kept reappearing without anyone connecting the dots.

What I did. Tracked recurring issue types across interactions and flagged them with specifics — not "customers are complaining," but 12 documented cases showing the same root cause. Surfaced product gaps and workflow inefficiencies directly to the right teams with suggested fixes.

Outcome
  • Recurring issues reached the product team faster, with better documentation
  • Several workflow changes implemented from patterns I identified
  • Reduced repeat contacts once root causes were fixed upstream
